Pollinators

General Information About Pollinators

There are many behind the scenes that occur in our daily lives from the environment to your food. The pollination process which helps produce food has components most do not realize. Pollinators come in many different sizes, and not doing well in our environment, for a number of reasons; insecticides, global warming, and our daily activities. If we do not do something about this soon, crops and food will start to rise in price and as a result more people will start to starve.

Declination of Pollinators In the World

Pollinators have continued to decline for a very long time. A prime example of this is how honey bee colonies have been decling at least 30% each year since 2006. Some pollinators more on a local level have started to go extinct. This is because of the land for agriculture needs to be cleared in order to be used. This takes away the ability for bees to create hives and nests. Without pollinators, farmers would have to pollinate by hand, like they do with vanilla. Vanilla was orginially grown in Mexico, however because of deforestation, there are no other birds or insects that can pollinate the vanilla plant.
